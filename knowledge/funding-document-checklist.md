# Funding Document Checklist

## Purpose

This file is the document-readiness source of truth for **Cash Flow Stress-Test Copilot**. It defines the core business, financial, disruption-specific, insurance, and use-of-funds documents that may support a lender, advisor, accountant, insurer, or internal review after a 13-week cash-flow stress test.

Use this file together with:

- `cash-flow-calculation-rules.md`
- `stress-test-scenario-methodology.md`
- `trigger-response-playbook.md`
- `lender-advisor-summary-template.md`
- `privacy-redaction-and-data-handling.md`

> **Planning boundary:** A complete document packet may improve organization and speed, but it does not guarantee funding, insurance payment, eligibility, approval, rates, terms, or professional acceptance.

---

# 1. Checklist Use Rules

The GPT should:

1. Ask what decision or conversation the documents will support.
2. Build a checklist matched to the business, disruption, and recipient.
3. Mark each item as `Available`, `Missing`, `Outdated`, `Not Applicable`, or `Needs Review`.
4. Explain why each missing document matters.
5. Avoid requesting sensitive credentials or unnecessary personal data.
6. Recommend secure storage and sharing.
7. Never claim a checklist is universally required by every lender or advisor.
8. Label provider-specific requirements as unknown until verified.

## Document Status Values

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Available | Current and ready to share |
| Missing | Not yet obtained |
| Outdated | Exists but may not cover the needed period |
| Not Applicable | Does not apply to this business or scenario |
| Needs Review | Exists but accuracy, completeness, or suitability is uncertain |

## Use-of-Funds Quality Checks

- [ ] Every amount connects to a forecast week.
- [ ] Every material amount has supporting documentation.
- [ ] Operating cash and financing proceeds are not double counted.
- [ ] The requested amount is not automatically equal to the funding gap.
- [ ] A contingency is explained rather than hidden.
- [ ] The recovery plan explains how the expense supports continued operations.
- [ ] The repayment discussion does not promise outcomes.
- [ ] Existing debts and obligations are accurately listed.

# 10. Document Readiness Score

Score one point for each item:

- [ ] Current bank statements are available.
- [ ] Current P&L is available.
- [ ] Current balance sheet is available.
- [ ] AR aging is available.
- [ ] AP aging is available.
- [ ] Debt schedule is complete.
- [ ] Payroll schedule is documented.
- [ ] Baseline forecast is reconciled.
- [ ] Scenario assumptions are documented.
- [ ] Use of funds is itemized.
- [ ] Recovery plan is documented.
- [ ] Disruption-specific evidence is organized.
- [ ] Sensitive information is redacted or securely stored.
- [ ] A one-page summary is ready.

| Score | Readiness |
|---|---|
| 0–4 | Major gaps |
| 5–8 | Partial packet |
| 9–11 | Conversation ready |
| 12–14 | Strongly organized |

The score measures organization, not approval probability.
